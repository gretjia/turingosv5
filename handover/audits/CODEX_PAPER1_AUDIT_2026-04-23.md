# CODEX Paper 1 Adversarial Audit - 2026-04-23

## STAT

- **STAT-1 - `p = 0.0195` is a one-sided exact McNemar/sign-test value, not a generic exact McNemar p-value.** The 4-seed memo reports 8 B-unique and 1 A-unique discordant cells and computes `(C(9,0)+C(9,1))/2^9 = 10/512 = 0.0195` (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:20-24`). That is the directional one-sided probability for B dominance; the two-sided exact value is `2 * 10/512 = 0.0391`. Action: label the test as one-sided with a pre-registered directional alternative, or report two-sided `p = 0.0391`. Reviewer stance: **accept with revision** if directional testing was pre-specified; **challenge** if presented as an omnibus exact test.

- **STAT-2 - Pooling 40 seed-by-problem trials overstates the effective sample size because the same 10 problem identities are reused across seeds.** The 4-seed table uses the same hard10 sample for seeds 141421, 31415, 2718, and 2357, with discordance concentrated in a few problem identities (`algebra_44`, `imo_1962_p2`, `algebra_apbon2pownleqapownpbpowon2`) plus one A-unique `algebra_332` (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:12-18`). This is not a Simpson reversal by seed, but it is a clustering problem: a problem-level sign summary has only about three positive problem clusters. Action: add a clustered sensitivity analysis, e.g. conditional/mixed logistic model with problem and seed effects, or a problem-cluster bootstrap. A mixed-effects model buys explicit seed/problem variance accounting, but with only 4 seeds it will mainly expose uncertainty, not prove robustness. Reviewer stance: **likely challenge**.

- **STAT-3 - The independent-sample cross-check is not significant, and multiplicity needs a declared family.** From the 4-seed counts `A = 7/40`, `B = 14/40` (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:18`), Fisher's exact test gives one-sided `p = 0.0631` and two-sided `p = 0.1263`; this does not replace McNemar, but it shows the result is fragile under alternative assumptions. If the paper treats hard10, easy10 control, per-seed dominance, and ablation as inferential claims, a 4-test Bonferroni threshold is `0.0125`, so one-sided `0.0195` fails. Action: designate one primary endpoint and move easy-set and ablation analyses to controls/exploratory, or apply Holm/Bonferroni. Reviewer stance: **likely challenge**.

## DESIGN

- **DESIGN-1 - The hard10 sample is enriched by construction and not a neutral draw from MiniF2F.** The draft says the 10 problems were selected because both Phase 9.A baseline seeds 31415 and 2718 failed them (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:147`), and the sample file says it is stratified across categories (`handover/evidence/sample_E1_hard10.txt:1-13`). The earlier verdict itself flags hard-set sample bias (`handover/ai-direct/E1_EMERGENCE_VERDICT_2026-04-23.md:88-93`). Action: report results on the full 36-problem hard pool or on a pre-registered random subset before claiming general hard-problem emergence. Reviewer stance: **likely challenge**.

- **DESIGN-2 - Matching `BOLTZMANN_SEED` controls the RNG seed, not the proof-state trajectory.** The draft lists the Boltzmann seed as matched per A/B pair (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:127-145`) and later claims this controls the dominant variance source (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:286-292`). But different prompts produce different accepted/rejected payloads, reputations, and partial states, so the same RNG stream does not imply the same parent selections after early divergence. Action: rephrase as a common-random-number control and add trajectory diagnostics: parent choices, accepted-node prefixes, and first-divergence transaction per pair. Reviewer stance: **likely challenge**.

- **DESIGN-3 - A simpler selection-bias explanation is broader tactic coverage, not swarm emergence.** A exposes only `ring, field_simp, linarith, nlinarith`, while B adds structural, rewriting, and Meta-Planner tactics (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:137-145`). The hard10 includes IMO, induction, olympiad, algebra, and AMC items (`handover/evidence/sample_E1_hard10.txt:1-13`). Action: add controls for homogeneous all-tactic prompt, homogeneous structural prompt, and single-agent/full-tactic prompt. Without these, `+200%` can be read as "the treatment was handed the needed tactic families." Reviewer stance: **likely challenge/reject for the emergence framing**.

## CAUSE

- **CAUSE-1 - Winning-agent attribution is correlational, not a mechanism identification.** The draft claims skill_3 solved 5/6 B-unique events and concludes Meta-Planner presence changes the routed state trajectory (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:181-196`); the supporting audit repeats this as the refined mechanism claim (`handover/evidence/ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT_2026-04-23.md:54-70`). The evidence identifies the final accepted agent, not whether prior Meta-Planner messages causally enabled the proof. Action: add causal traces showing which Meta-Planner messages were consumed before the winning node, or replay B with those messages removed. Reviewer stance: **likely reject the mechanism claim as written**.

- **CAUSE-2 - The completed no-Meta ablation weakens the "Meta-Planner is the mechanism" claim.** On seed 141421, A solves 1/10, the no-Meta 3-skill ablation solves 2/10, and full B solves 3/10 (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:37-49`). That supports "Meta-Planner may add `imo_1962_p2` in this seed," not "Meta-Planner, not arbitrary heterogeneity, is the mechanism." Action: run the ablation across all 4 seeds and test B vs ablation with paired discordant counts. Reviewer stance: **likely reject causal attribution; accept as exploratory observation**.

- **CAUSE-3 - The paper's own 4-seed update says generic heterogeneity contributes.** The final memo says the claim should shift to "both generic heterogeneity AND Meta-Planner role contribute" (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:51-60`) and later says generic heterogeneity accounts for `algebra_44`-class gains (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:84-86`). Action: delete "not arbitrary prompt diversity" from the abstract/contributions and replace it with "mechanism remains underdetermined; one ablation suggests both non-meta role diversity and Meta-Planner may matter." Reviewer stance: **accept with major revision**.

## LEAKAGE

- **LEAKAGE-1 - The "symmetric prompt specificity" defense is false on a token-level comparison.** A's skill prompt names 4 local algebra tactics; B contains A plus skill_1 (`induction, cases, rcases, constructor`), skill_2 (`simp, norm_num, rw, calc`), and skill_3 (`by_contra`, `induction'`, `refine ⟨?_, ?_⟩`) plus policy text about "TACTIC FAMILY SHIFT" and rejecting "another small step" (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:137-145`). The limitation says this is symmetric with skill_0 (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:298-305`), but B has a larger tactic vocabulary and a strategy heuristic. Action: use tactic-vocabulary-matched prompts before claiming prompt-only role heterogeneity rather than tactic leakage. Reviewer stance: **likely challenge**.

- **LEAKAGE-2 - Winning payloads reuse treatment-provided tactics, which a hostile reviewer will call proof-skeleton leakage.** The draft's example B-unique payload uses `constructor`, `refine`, and `nlinarith`, then says A agents essentially never emit `constructor` or `refine` because skill_0 does not name them (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:198-210`). This is consistent with B being given the missing skeleton tactics, not necessarily with emergent collective intelligence. Action: add no-tactic Meta-Planner, all-tactic homogeneous, and shuffled-tactic controls. Reviewer stance: **likely challenge/reject for strong emergence language**.

- **LEAKAGE-3 - The ablation needed to resolve leakage is explicitly future work in the draft.** The paper says formalizing the tactic-name leakage concern via ablation is future work (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:303-305`), while the supporting audit asserts symmetry without that ablation (`handover/evidence/ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT_2026-04-23.md:32-35`). Action: either run the tactic-name ablations before submission or demote leakage-sensitive claims to "hypothesis-generating." Reviewer stance: **likely challenge**.

## REPRO

- **REPRO-1 - Missing per-row build provenance is not fatal for Lean proof validity, but it is a systems-paper reproducibility defect.** The draft discloses missing per-row `build_sha` (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:258-260`), and the audit says all original 80 rows had `build_sha = None` (`handover/evidence/ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT_2026-04-23.md:28-35`). The draft provides external commit references (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:315-322`), which helps but does not prove each row came from that binary. Action: regenerate the published table rows with row-level `build_sha`, container digest, and command line, or clearly mark the old rows as legacy provenance. Reviewer stance: **accept with revision for proof claims; challenge for systems reproducibility**.

- **REPRO-2 - The evidence index is stale relative to the 4-seed final claim.** The evidence README maps only the original 8 E1 batches (`handover/evidence/README.md:18-30`), while the 4-seed memo adds seed 2357 and ablation JSONLs (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:90-98`). Action: update the archive index so a reviewer can reproduce the exact 4-seed claim without inferring which files supersede which. Reviewer stance: **accept with revision**.

- **REPRO-3 - Single-node accepted proofs undercut the "multi-agent tape chain" narrative unless separated from the solve-set claim.** The audit discloses that many B solves have `gp_node_count = 1` and are multi-line proofs in a single `step` call (`handover/evidence/ADVERSARIAL_AUDIT_2026-04-23.md:32-35`); the draft repeats that the multi-agent chain story applies only to a subset (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:298-305`). Raw examples include B `mathd_algebra_332` with `gp_node_count = 1` in seed 31415 and 2718 (`handover/evidence/e1_jsonl/E1_B_seed31415_n8_20260424T012605.jsonl:3`, `handover/evidence/e1_jsonl/E1_B_seed2718_n8_20260424T024005.jsonl:3`). Action: report node-count distributions and state that the primary empirical claim is solve-set dominance, not universal multi-agent proof composition. Reviewer stance: **accept with revision if narrowed; challenge if narrative remains broad**.

## CLAIM

- **CLAIM-1 - Strongest defensible primary claim.** A defensible version is: "On a selected 10-problem MiniF2F hard set, with the same model and transaction cap, a 4-role prompt condition solved more paired seed-by-problem instances than a homogeneous algebra-only prompt condition: 14/40 vs 7/40, with 8 B-only and 1 A-only discordant events; the directional exact sign test is `p = 0.0195` and the two-sided value is `p = 0.0391`." This is grounded in the final 4-seed table (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:8-24`). The easy-set delta `0` can be reported as a saturated negative control, not proof of compositional specificity (`handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:26-33`). Reviewer stance: **accept with major narrowing**.

- **CLAIM-2 - Ranking the section 1.2 contributions by defensibility.** Most defensible: **#4 Methodological discipline**, because the draft names the discovered telemetry bugs and fixes (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:39-41`, `handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:277-284`). Next: **#1 Experimental demonstration**, but it must be updated from 3 seeds/strict containment to 4 seeds/one A-unique and directional testing (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:33-37`, `handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:64-78`). Third: **#3 Verifiable substrate**, because traceability/test claims may be true but the "first application" novelty claim is unsupported in the draft's empty references section (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:39`, `handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:372-429`). Least defensible: **#2 Mechanism identification**, because the ablation and final memo contradict "not arbitrary prompt diversity" (`handover/ai-direct/PAPER_1_FULL_DRAFT_2026-04-23.md:37`, `handover/ai-direct/E1_FINAL_4SEEDS_2026-04-23.md:37-60`). Action: demote #2 to exploratory, soften #1, and either support or remove the "first application" novelty in #3. Reviewer stance: **likely reject #2 as written; challenge #1/#3; accept #4 with documentation**.

## Verdict

**CHALLENGE** - The data support a narrow prompt-heterogeneity improvement on a selected hard set, but the current draft overclaims statistical strength, causal mechanism, prompt-leakage symmetry, and swarm-intelligence emergence.
