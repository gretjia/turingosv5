## 1. Goal

Close TB-1's deferred runtime boundary. A real `WorkTx` MUST traverse `Sequencer::dispatch_transition` and produce one of two ledger outcomes:

**Accepted WorkTx**:
- passes WorkTx pure validation in `dispatch_transition` (parent-root match, predicate bundle true, monetary invariants hold);
- passes RSP-1 admission (YES stake > 0; escrow exists for `task_id`);
- returns `q_next`;
- is committed by `Sequencer::apply_one` to the **canonical L4 transition ledger** (`bottom_white::ledger::transition_ledger` + `LedgerWriter`, NOT `economy::ledger::AcceptedLedger`);
- advances `state_root_t`, `ledger_root_t`, accepted `logical_t`.

**Rejected WorkTx** (predicate-fail / parent-root mismatch / stakeless / no escrow / monetary violation / `NotYetImplemented`-class):
- is recorded in the L4.E rejection-evidence ledger (`bottom_white::ledger::rejection_evidence`) keyed by `submit_id`;
- does NOT consume accepted `logical_t`;
- does NOT mutate `state_root_t` or `ledger_root_t`.

## 8. Three declarative success proofs (v2 — proof 3 added per r1 P0-E)

TB-2 is provisionally green when all three are demonstrable end-to-end through `Sequencer::submit` (NOT through bespoke test-only constructors that bypass the runtime spine):

**Proof 1 — rejection spine**:
> A predicate-failed WorkTx submitted through `Sequencer::submit` does not advance `logical_t`, does not change `state_root_t` or `ledger_root_t`, and creates exactly one L4.E record with the same `submit_id` returned by the receipt. (Tests I3 + I7.)

**Proof 2 — acceptance spine**:
> A predicate-passing WorkTx with positive `stake` and seeded escrow (via P0-B option (a) bridge) advances `state_root_t` to exactly `sha256(WORKTX_ACCEPT_DOMAIN_V1 ‖ prev_state_root ‖ canonical_hash(tx))`, advances `ledger_root_t`, increments accepted `logical_t`, and creates zero L4.E records. (Tests I9 + I10 + I11 + I12.)

**Proof 3 — replay invariant (P1:8 / Art IV Boot)**:
> After submitting one accepted and one rejected WorkTx, reconstructing `QState` from the canonical `transition_ledger` ALONE (using the existing `replay_full_transition` machinery) yields the same `state_root_t` as the live sequencer. L4.E records do NOT participate in replay; rejected submissions leave the canonical accepted spine untouched. (Test I13.)

Only after all three proofs are green can the project honestly claim:

> TuringOS runtime kernel honors the L4 / L4.E split.

Until then the honest claim remains:

> TuringOS has the primitives required to honor the L4 / L4.E split.
